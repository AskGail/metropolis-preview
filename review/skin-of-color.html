01 · What it is

What dermatology for skin of color means

Skin of color, broadly Fitzpatrick types IV through VI and much of type III, carries more active melanin. That single fact changes how skin conditions look, how they are diagnosed, and how they respond to treatment. Dermatology for skin of color is care that accounts for those differences at every step, rather than applying a one-size protocol built around lighter skin.

It is not a separate specialty. It is dermatology practiced with the training, the experience, and the equipment to serve every skin tone equally well.

02 · Why it's different

Why skin of color needs different expertise, not different marketing

This is the part most practices skip. The clinical realities that make experience matter Dr. Higgins: confirm the mechanisms to emphasize and correct any wording:

  • Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation. Inflammation that would fade on lighter skin can leave lasting dark marks on deeper skin, so treatment has to minimize inflammation, not just address the original concern. Dr. Higgins: confirm
  • Device and laser selection. Many light-based devices target pigment, and on darker skin the wrong wavelength or setting can burn or cause hypo- or hyperpigmentation. Choosing the right device is a safety decision, not a preference. Dr. Higgins: confirm the device categories we can and cannot use
  • Scarring tendency. Keloid and hypertrophic scarring are more common in some skin-of-color patients, which shapes how procedures are planned. Dr. Higgins: confirm
  • Diagnosis. Redness and some conditions can present differently on deeper skin, raising the risk of missed or delayed diagnosis without an experienced eye. Dr. Higgins: confirm

The through-line: on skin of color, avoiding harm is as much of the skill as delivering the result. That is what experience buys.

03 · Who it's for

Who this care is for

Patients with medium to deep skin tones who want a dermatologist who has genuinely treated skin like theirs, whether the concern is medical or cosmetic. It is a fit if you are dealing with:

  • Dark marks or uneven tone after acne, ingrown hairs, or irritation (post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation)
  • Melasma or stubborn pigmentation
  • Keloid or raised scars
  • Acne and acne scarring on deeper skin
  • An interest in cosmetic treatments and a wariness about whether they are safe for your skin tone
