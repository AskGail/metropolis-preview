Lasers & resurfacing

LaseMD Ultra laser skin resurfacing

Resurfacing that evens tone and smooths texture with little downtime, tuned by our dermatologists to your skin tone, not just some skin tones.

01 · What it is

What LaseMD Ultra is

LaseMD Ultra is a non-ablative fractional laser made by Cynosure Lutronic. It uses a 1927nm thulium wavelength to renew the surface of the skin without removing it, which is why it improves tone and texture with far less downtime than aggressive resurfacing.

"Non-ablative" is the key word: rather than stripping the outer layer, it works underneath it. That is also what makes it, in trained hands, one of the more forgiving resurfacing lasers across a range of skin tones.

02 · How it works

How it works, and what it's cleared to treat

The 1927nm wavelength is absorbed by water in the upper layers of the skin (to roughly 200 microns deep), creating thousands of microscopic treatment zones. Those trigger the skin's natural repair, stimulating fresh collagen and a clearer, more even surface. A paired cosmeceutical delivery step can drive topicals in immediately afterward.

LaseMD Ultra is FDA-cleared to treat sun spots, age spots, and actinic keratoses. Many patients also choose it to even out overall tone and texture. Your Metropolis dermatologist will talk through what it can realistically do for your skin.

03 · Who it's for

Who it's a good fit for

  • Sun damage, uneven tone, and early pigment changes
  • Rough or uneven texture and enlarged-looking pores
  • Anyone wanting a reset with minimal downtime
  • Patients across the Fitzpatrick range, with settings customized to skin tone
Skin of color

LaseMD Ultra on skin of color

Because it is non-ablative and uses the 1927nm wavelength, LaseMD Ultra is one of the more forgiving resurfacing options for deeper skin tones, Fitzpatrick IV to VI, when the settings are matched to your skin.

On melanin-rich skin, the risk to manage is post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation. We lower it with conservative, customized settings, and the plan is guided by our dermatologists who focus on skin of color. It is care tuned to your skin, not a one-setting-fits-all pass.

05 · Risks & recovery

What to expect, and the risks we plan around

Typical after-effects are 1 to 2 days of redness, then a fine, dry, sandpaper-like texture and light flaking for up to a week as the skin renews. There is usually no mandatory downtime, though your skin looks flushed and feels gritty during that week, and stronger treatments take a little longer.

On deeper skin tones, the main risk to manage is temporary pigment change (post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation). This is exactly why device settings and provider experience matter, and why we treat skin of color with customized, conservative settings.

Individual results vary. This page is educational and not medical advice.
